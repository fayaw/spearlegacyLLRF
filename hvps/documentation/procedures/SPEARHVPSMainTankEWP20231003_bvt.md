## Hazard Analysis (add rows as needed)


| Step or Task | Step or Task Description | Hazard (include shock and arc flash hazard information from the hazard label affixed to the equipment or from the electrical analysis engineer) | Control (include electrical safety PPE based on the electrical hazard information) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  | Electrical: Isolate energy sources to both SPEAR HVPSs. | 90 kV / 27 A, DC 12.47 kVAC mains 120 VAC for oil pump 125 VDC control power for main tank interlocks 8 kJ stored in capacitors, 90 kV | Perform “SPEAR3 RF HVPS Energy Isolation Plan” (SR-444-636-02-R1).  This locks out energy sources and grounds the output for both SPEAR HVPSs. Note, if planning to perform maintenance on a supply while the other is turned-on, an additional procedure that describes the correct lockout condition and the removal/shorting of the output cable from the switch tank is required and must be prepared.. |
|  | Mechanical: Prepare work area | Trip/Slip | Upon entering the top of the supply, familiarize self with trip hazards and clean up and oil and dirt. |
|  | Mechanical: Remove cables/conduits | Trips, prohibit lid removal Oxygen deficiency | -Valve off and remove nitrogen line. -Apply clamshell with administrative lock and tag to HVPS lid connection end of nitrogen line. Tag should indicate “Do Not Connect”   -Disconnect cables and remove over-pressure/under-pressure conduit.   -Disconnect cabling to rapid pressure release valve. |
|  | Electrical: Work within the main tank | 90 kV, 0.22 uF output cap 30 kV, 4 each: 8 uF filter caps  30 kV, 4 each: 0.24 uF crowbar SCR snubbers | Execute all electrical steps of the ‘Work with the SPEAR HVPS crowbar tank” EIP.  This includes the three steps required to safe the filter caps and output cap and ground the output cap. |
|  | Electrical: Work within the main tank | 12.7 kV, 12 stacks with 0.30 uF snubbers (2 x .015 uF) | Execute all electrical steps of the ‘Work with the SPEAR HVPS phase tank” EIP.   Alternatively, may be able to use two Phenix discharge sticks to soft and then hard short between at least one side of each filter inductor and a point on the primary magnetics.  This would be done once access is made into the main tank. |
|  | Mechanical: Remove oil from tank | Removing 1200 gallons of vegetable derived oil is a spill hazard | If transferring oil outside of building 514 secondary containment then oil containers must be stored in another means of secondary containment.  Workers must have received OJT. Required completed SLAC training on worker’s STA includes: 103: Hazard Communication; 105/R Hazardous Waste Management and 169: Spill Prevention and Response Training. Removing more than 1200 gallons can expose the power supply magnetics to air, risking permanent damage of the HVPS.  There are 2600 gallons of oil in the main tank. |
|  | Mechanical: Remove low and/or high side main tank cover | Cover could drop unexpectedly during move with chain hoist.  Covers weigh ~300 lbs each. | Chain hoist with spreader bar must be used to move covers.  The chain hoist,spreader bar and crane frame safety inspection must be current.  The operators must be trained in ES&H courses 280 and 280 PRA.  The operators must have an approved ordinary lifting plan.  Wear safety toe, non-slip shoes, hard hat and durable work gloves when using chain hoist. |
|  |  |  |  |
|  | Electrical: Work on oil pump or within 1 foot of AC terminal block | 120 VAC shock hazard. | ZVV incoming AC to pump at terminal block. |
|  | Mechanical: Transporting heavy or awkward parts to and from the top of the power supplies. | Strain and trip hazard carrying awkward/heavy items up and down the stairs from the top of the power supply. | The SSRL mechanical services group (MSG) has a lifting basket staged next to the HVPS.  Bulky and/or awkward components that need to be transported to and from the top of the HVPS are lifted in the basket by a forklift operated by the MSG.  Workers on the HVPS should coordinate the movement of these components with MSG. |
