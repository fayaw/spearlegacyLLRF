| G. Normal Shutdown Procedure |
| --- |
| 1. Notify Spear Operations that the RF HVPS needs to locked and tagged and so that they can notify the High Voltage Electricians. |
| 2. After the High Voltage Electricians open the 12.47kV disconnect switch and install grounds on the busses lock and tag the 12.47 KV disconnect switch. |
| 3. View a zero output voltage verification by looking at the local touch panel display. Proceed to B132, depress the manual plunger and on the termination tank, and lock and tag. |
| 4. Proceed to B117. Open and lock and tag Panel LPB CB20. |
| 5. Proceed to B514. Open and lock and tag the 208 V disconnect switch and the 120 Vdc circuit breaker. |
| 6. Verify affected areas are clear of personnel. Perform ZZV as required before starting work. When working inside any of the tanks ZVV with ground sticks before making contact with high voltage components. |
| 7. Re-energize the equipment. Note: be certain to consider effects of re-energization on all systems downstream of energy source. |
| 8. Notify supervisor when work is complete. |
| 9. If you find any errors in this procedure, or have suggestions on how to improve it, provide your comments to the equipment custodian. |




| H. Startup Procedure |
| --- |
| 1.	Remove lock and tag from the 12.47 KVac disconnect switch. Notify SPEAR Operations that the RF HVPS is ready to return to operation and ask them to contact the High Voltage Electricians to remove the grounds and close the disconnect switch. |
| 2.	Once the High Voltage Electricians have restored the 12.47 kVac proceed to B117 to remove lock and tag and energize Panel LPB CB20. |
| 3.	Proceed to B514. Remove lock and tag and energize the 208 Vac disconnect switch and the 120 Vdc circuit breaker. |
| 4.	Proceed to B132. Remove lock and tag and lift up the manual grounding plunger. |
| 5.	Return control of the RF HVPS to operations. |
