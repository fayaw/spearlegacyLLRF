## Hazard Analysis (add rows as needed)


| Step or Task | Step or Task Description | Hazard (include shock and arc flash hazard information from the hazard label affixed to the equipment or from the electrical analysis engineer) | Control (include electrical safety PPE based on the electrical hazard information) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  | Electrical: Isolate energy sources to both SPEAR HVPSs. | 90 kV / 27 A, DC 8 kJ stored in capacitors, 90 kV | Perform “SPEAR3 RF HVPS Energy Isolation Plan” (SR-444-636-02-R1).  This locks out energy sources and grounds the output for both SPEAR HVPSs. Note, if planning to perform maintenance on a supply while the other is turned-on, an additional procedure that describes the correct lockout condition and the removal/shorting of the output cable from the switch tank is required and must be prepared. |
|  | Mechanical: Remove oil from tank | 135 gallons of vegetable derived oil is a spill hazard | If transferring oil outside of building 514 secondary containment then oil containers must be stored in another means of secondary containment.  Workers must have received OJT.  Required completed SLAC training on worker’s STA includes: 103: Hazard Communication; 105/R Hazardous Waste Management and 169: Spill Prevention and Response Training. |
|  | Mechanical: Prepare work area | Trip/Slip | Upon entering the top of the supply, familiarize self with trip hazards and clean up oil and dirt. |
|  | Mechanical: Remove nitrogen supply line | Oxygen deficiency | -Valve off and remove nitrogen supply line from main tank lid -Apply clamshell with administrative lock and tag to HVPS lid connection end of nitrogen line. Tag should indicate “Do Not Connect” |
|  | Mechanical: Remove crowbar tank cover plate | Muscle strain (~70 lbs lift hazard) | Must have 2 people to remove cover. Wear safety toe, non-slip shoes and durable work gloves. |
|  | Electrical: Work within crowbar tank. | 90 kV, 0.22 uF output cap | Use 1 dedicated Phenix discharge stick, rated for 100 kVDC, to soft ground and then hard ground the top of the 0.22 uF output capacitor. |
|  | Electrical: Work within the crowbar tank | 30 kV, 4 each: 8 uF filter caps  30 kV, 4 each: 0.24 uF SCR snubbers | Use 2 dedicated Phenix soft grounding discharge sticks to simultaneously ground across each of four bushing sets feeding the main tank.  Use additional ground sticks to apply hard grounds. |
|  | Electrical: Work within the crowbar tank | Dielectric recharge 90 kV, 0.22 uF output cap | Bolt a shorting wire from the top of 0.22 uF output capacitor to ground. |
|  | Mechanical: Work in crowbar tank | Bumps, bruises, and strain.  Exposure to high voltage oil. | Must have physical ability to safely perform the work.  Removal of SCR stacks is particularly difficult.  Requires foam/rubber pads, maintenance blankets.  Use disposable nitrile gloves when handling oil-covered components. Note: should rotate stacks long ways to drain captured oil from them over tank before moving them to side.  After removing stacks from the tank, place them in plastic parts bins lined with oil absorbent pads. Proper waste containers from Waste Management must be obtained before the start of work.  All material used for handling oil-covered items, such as nitrile gloves, absorbent pads, wipes, etc., must be placed in these containers. |
|  | Mechanical: Transporting heavy or awkward parts to and from the top of the power supplies. | Strain and trip hazard carrying awkward/heavy items up and down the stairs from the top of the power supply. | The SSRL mechanical services group (MSG) has a lifting basket staged next to the HVPS.  Bulky and/or awkward components that need to be transported to and from the top of the HVPS are lifted in the basket by a forklift operated by the MSG.  Workers on the HVPS should coordinate the movement of these components with MSG. |
