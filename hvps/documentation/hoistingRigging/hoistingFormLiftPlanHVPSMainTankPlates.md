This form is used to document the lift plan required for ordinary lifts. The plan must be approved by someone with knowledge and experience in hoisting and rigging, either the supervisor, the supervisor’s designee, the ESH coordinator, or the hoisting and rigging program manager if requested. Deviations from the approved plan must be reviewed by the original approver. A copy of the plan must be kept at the work site (see  [SLAC-I-730-0A21S-060]).
If the lift is covered by an existing procedure, attach it to the form and fill out only first page. 
Approved plans may be reused for similar lifts of like material. The location, time, and workers may be different. Plans must be signed only once: by preparers and approvers when being written and approved and by workers after reading the plan. Plans must be revised and reapproved if conditions change.

| Characterize the Task (include directions for lifting, rotation, flipping, speeds, and travel) |
| --- |
| Using the rigging outlined in this plan, a chain hoist, an adjustable lifting beam, lifting straps, shackles, and eyebolts, lift the large steel plate covering either half of the main tank of either HVPS1 or HVPS2.  Before raising the main tank plate, apply full load to eye bolts by removing slack on lift system components.  Inspect welded eye bolts for cracks or other visibly recognizable signs that raise safety concerns.  First prepare the fixed main tank lid by placing 8 inches of timbers on top of that lid to support the lid to be moved.  After unbolting the lid to be moved from the main tank, lift and move the lid over the timbers on the main tank plate still in place.  Then lower it onto these supports.  After the work inside of the open half of the HVPS main tank is completed, reverse the process to install the plate back onto the HVPS. |



| Evaluate the Hazards (define specific controls) |
| --- |
| Equipment falling: Crane, load, rigging, fixtures, or other mechanical parts could fail resulting in damage to the load or injury to personnel. Be aware of your surroundings and pay attention for tell-tale sounds of failure. General mitigations for all lifts: PPE: Safety glasses, hard hat, steel toe shoes, and gloves (at a minimum). Ensure ES&H training required is up to date. Only qualified operator can perform the lift. Ensure crane inspection is current. Ensure all rigging is inspected and in good working order. Ensure the load is properly secured. Use spotters for both watching the lift and keeping unauthorized personnel away from the lift area. Install rigging gear properly paying attention to sling angles. Do not place any body part under the load. |
